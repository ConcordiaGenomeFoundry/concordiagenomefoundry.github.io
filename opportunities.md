    <h1>
    <strong>
    Classification: Research Assistant under the CARE union at Concordia University<br>
    Salary: $27.37/hr<br>
    Weekly Hours: 35<br>
    Duration: 1 year, renewable<br>
    Start Date: as of August 2022<br>
    Principal Investigator or Immediate Supervisor: Dr. Vincent Martin<br>
    Location: 7141 Sherbrooke St West, GE building, Montreal, QC, H4B 1R6<br>
    Unit: Centre for Applied Synthetic Biology at Concordia University<br>
    </strong><br>
    <strong>Scope<br><br></strong>
    The Concordia Centre for Applied Synthetic Biology (CASB) is Canada’s first research centre focused on Synthetic 
    Biology. The Centre houses the <a style="color: white;" 
    href="https://www.concordia.ca/research/genome-foundry.html">Genome Foundry</a> for Synthetic Biology, a unique 
    CFI- and NRC-funded technology platform that aspires to accelerate the design-build-test cycle of biological 
    engineering <a style="color: white;" href="http://www.concordia.ca/casb">(www.concordia.ca/casb)</a>.<br>
    <br>
    The Centre has an urgent need for multiple Research Assistants with a background in applied synthetic biology. 
    The Research Assistants are needed to support the microbial genome engineering activities of the Genome Foundry. 
    These activities include but are not limited to high-throughput genome editing using the Genome Foundry’s 
    automation and LIMS platforms.<br>
    <br>
    The Research Assistants will report to the Microbial Suite Platform Coordinator, Nicholas Gold, and set priorities 
    in consultation with the Centre Director, Dr. Vincent Martin, and Genome Foundry Platform Coordinators.<br>
    <br>
    These are key positions of the Genome Foundry; the Research Assistants will need to display a high level of 
    initiative, drive, and accountability.<br>
    <br>
    Primary responsibilities<br><br>
    <ul>
        <li>Coordinate work and report to the Platform Coordinators to develop and streamline workflows.</li>
        <li>Perform validated day-to-day operations in support of platform research activities.</li>
        <li>Responsible for operating and maintaining automated instrumentation.</li>
        <li>Responsible for upkeep of inventory (consumables, DNA stocks, strain collection).</li>
        <li>Work in interdisciplinary teams and collaborate with scientists on several projects in parallel.</li>
    </ul>
    <br>
    Qualifications<br><br>
    <ul>
        <li>Master’s or equivalent qualification in Biology or relevant field.</li>
        <li>Minimum of three years of relevant work experience or the equivalent combination of education and experience.</li>
        <li>Proficiency in current molecular biology techniques (e.g. CRISPR, Golden Gate, homologous recombination).</li>
        <li>Experience with yeast culturing and genetics is an asset.</li>
        <li>Experience with laboratory automation an asset.</li>
        <li>Experience with next-generation sequencing library preparation an asset.</li>
        <li>Experience with programing (Python or R) is an asset.</li>
    </ul>
